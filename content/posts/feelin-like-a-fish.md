I love this ad by the [Ad Council](http://www.adcouncil.org/search.aspx) concerning children with Asthma. I love how expressive both the copy and the image, they go together so well. I saw this ad while shopping for jeans at a local mall. It was executed so well that I felt I had to take a picture with my iPhone. It wasn't the best of "snaps" but it got the job done while I was in a crowded mall.

[![](http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_ZrvRLk7aCg4/SbwzpKX1SmI/AAAAAAAAAGY/Lju10pbQqek/s400/fishad2.jpg)](http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_ZrvRLk7aCg4/SbwzpKX1SmI/AAAAAAAAAGY/Lju10pbQqek/s1600-h/fishad2.jpg)

This outdoor ad is part of a campaign sponsored by the [U.S. Environmental Protection Agency](http://www.epa.gov) is called [No Attacks](http://insidethatad.blogspot.com/www.noattacks.org). The campaign will use many different advertising vehicles including: print, TV, outdoor, and and interactive. Interactive is an especially important medium for today's younger generation.

The ad agency that volunteered for this campaign is [Campbell-Ewald](http://www.campbell-ewald.com/) I have to give the Detroit based agency props for taking this assignment. 

As an Asthma sufferer myself, I think this is an absolutely great campaign to be a part of.
