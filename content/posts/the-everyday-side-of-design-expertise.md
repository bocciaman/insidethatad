When you think of contemporary product designers, people like Philippe  Starck or Jonathan Ive come to mind. We've come to see designers as  larger-than-life entities, who from some Olympian perch survey the  foibles and anxieties of the masses (at least, those with some  disposable income) before setting the Chinese factories in motion to  crank out yet another disposable bauble. The [Fixperts project](http://fixperts.org/) features a very different side to the practice of design, having as its  objective the creation of "meaningful content that showcases how the  design process can solve everyday challenges." Solving everyday  challenges. What a concept!
