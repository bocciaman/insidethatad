Mountain Dew has recently introduced a new berry-flavored drink that they hope will saturate the web. The campaign known as “Voltage Everywhere” is primarily a [viral marketing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viral_marketing) effort on the part of [PepsiCo](http://www.pepsico.com). The “Voltage Everywhere” campaign is asking people to go to [Dewmocracyvoltage.com](http://www.dewmocracyvoltage.com) where dew fans are rewarded points for spreading the word about the new soft drink.

By spreading the word I mean talking it up on social networks like [Facebook](http://www.facebook.com) and [MySpace](http://www.myspace.com) just to name a few. They can place notes on message boards and start fan pages. Fans can then collect points towards things like an [Xbox](http://www.xbox.com) or [iPod](http://www.apple.com/itunes/).  This type of promotion is the year 2009 version of school promotions such as “look under the cap to win.”

On [Dewmocracyvoltage.com](http://www.dewmocracyvoltage.com) fans can also play games with other site members, download wallpaper, and do many other things. The site intends to keep the target audience engaged with the product. This is a great idea in my opinion, but it is already being executed on many other websites like the ones mentioned above, why would a consumer want to add another site that they have to visit?

The creators of the site, [DDB Tribal](http://www.tribalddb.com/) claim it is so that the consumer can become deeply involved with the product. Would someone really want to be that engaged with his or her favorite soft drink? [DDB Tribal](http://www.tribalddb.com/) seems to think this is a great idea. 

 By the way [DDB Tribal](http://www.tribalddb.com) is an interactive creative shop.
